Study Notes

Romans 12:14–21 teaches us how Christians should treat others — even our enemies.
v.14–16 — Bless those who hurt you. Share joy and sorrow with others. Live in harmony.
v.17–18 — Never repay evil with evil. As much as possible, live at peace with everyone.
v.19–20 — Don't take revenge. Leave justice to God. Instead, show kindness to your enemy.
v.21 — The great summary: Overcome evil with good.
Key theme: love conquers evil — through blessing, peace, and good deeds.
📌 한글 해설
로마서 12:14–21은 그리스도인이 다른 사람들을, 심지어 원수까지 어떻게 대해야 하는지 가르쳐 줍니다.
14–16절 — 너희를 박해하는 자들을 축복하라. 즐거워하는 자와 우는 자와 함께하라. 서로 마음을 같이하라.
17–18절 — 악으로 악을 갚지 말라. 할 수 있는 대로 모든 사람과 평화하라.
19–20절 — 원수를 갚지 말고 하나님께 맡기라. 오히려 원수에게 친절을 베풀라.
21절 — 위대한 요약: 선으로 악을 이기라.
핵심 주제: 사랑이 악을 이긴다 — 축복, 평화, 그리고 선행을 통하여.

📖 Week 2: Adam and Eve Genesis 2–3 ▸
Theme: Sin makes a mess of everything
📚 The Story Genesis 2–3 ▸

God created the first man, Adam, and the first woman, Eve, on the sixth day. They lived with God in the beautiful Garden of Eden. God told them they could eat the fruit of every tree in the garden — except one: the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.

One day, a serpent came to Eve and asked, "Did God really say you must not eat from that tree?" The serpent made Eve doubt God. Eve and Adam both ate the fruit. This was the first sin — and it was why God sent Adam and Eve out of the Garden of Eden.

💬 God's Message ▸

Sin broke Adam and Eve's perfect relationship with God. Since then, we have all sinned and we are all separated from God. But there is good news! God loves us so much that He sent His only Son, Jesus.

Sin still makes a mess of the world. But God found a way to fix what is broken — through Jesus.

🎨 Activity — Sin Makes a Mess! ▸
🧴 Materials: Toothpaste, paper plate

Squeeze about one inch of toothpaste onto the plate. Now try to put it back in the tube and clean it all up.

You can't! — Just like sin.

Adam and Eve's disobedience shows us that sin's effects cannot be reversed — just like toothpaste cannot go back in the tube. It affects our lives and the lives of others around us.

Good news: We have a Savior — Jesus — who can make right what sin has broken. ✦
