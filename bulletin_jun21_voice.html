Bible Study
Matthew 7 : 15 – 20
"True and False Prophets"

Matthew 7:15–20 is part of the Sermon on the Mount. Jesus warns his disciples about false prophets — those who appear righteous on the outside but are inwardly destructive. The test he gives is simple and profound: look at the fruit.

1. The warning (v. 15)
False prophets come dressed in sheep's clothing — they look harmless, even godly. But inwardly they are ravenous wolves. Jesus does not say they might appear — he says they will come. Spiritual discernment is not optional.

2. The test: fruit (vv. 16–19)
A tree is known by what it produces. Thornbushes do not yield grapes; thistles do not yield figs. No matter how impressive the appearance, the inner nature will eventually reveal itself through the fruit of a person's life — their character, their teaching, their effect on others.

3. The conclusion — this week's memorization verse ★ (v. 20)
"Thus you will recognize them by their fruits." This is the summary and the call. We are not to be naïve. We are to be discerning — watching what a teacher's life, words, and fruit actually produce over time.

The good news: a good tree bears good fruit. Those who are truly rooted in Christ will show it — in love, in truth, in sacrifice. Pray for discernment, and pray to be a good tree yourself.
